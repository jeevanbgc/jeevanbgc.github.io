Generative AI for Membrane Partitioning — One-Third the Compute

Predicting how drugs partition into lipid membranes is central to understanding absorption, distribution, and off-target effects. Fully converged molecular dynamics (MD) umbrella sampling gives accurate answers but is expensive — often requiring extensive sampling for each compound.

In our recent work, we explored whether generative AI could fill the gaps. Denoising diffusion probabilistic models (DDPMs) trained on sparse umbrella-sampling data from MD simulations were able to generate additional conformations and improve partition coefficient predictions.

Key takeaway

By combining DDPMs with targeted MD, we reduced the computational cost to roughly one-third of a full umbrella-sampling campaign while maintaining reasonable accuracy for membrane partitioning predictions.

Why it matters

As generative models mature in chemistry and biophysics, the opportunity is not to replace physics-based simulation but to use it more efficiently — letting MD focus sampling where it matters most and letting AI interpolate where data is sparse.
