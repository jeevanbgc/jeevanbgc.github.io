Research

Computational biophysics, AI, and molecular modeling across academia and industry.

Overview

My research combines physics-based molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, machine learning, statistical physics, and quantum mechanics to understand the structure, dynamics, and function of biomolecules — proteins, membranes, and small molecules.

Current Focus — Peptide Design (AbbVie)

Computational and AI-driven approaches to peptide design, applying generative and physics-based modeling methods to therapeutic peptide discovery and optimization.

Previous Focus — Protein Degradation & Drug Discovery (Dana-Farber)

At the Center for Protein Degradation, my work centered on:

  • Computational chemistry & biophysics applied to small molecules targeting therapeutically important proteins
  • Single-particle cryo-EM data analysis integrated with computational modeling
  • Structure-based drug design workflows connecting simulation to experimental structural biology

PhD Research — Computational Biophysics (FIU)

Working with Drs. Prem P. Chapagain and Bernard S. Gerstman, my doctoral research explored:

  • Transformer proteins and conformational transformation — MD simulations of RfaH α-helix to β-barrel transitions
  • Ebola virus matrix protein VP40 — membrane interactions and viral budding
  • Membrane protein and GPCR ligand binding — lipid bilayer effects on ligand access

Methods & Tools

  • Molecular dynamics simulation (all-atom and coarse-grained, e.g., Martini force field)
  • Free energy / PMF calculations, including umbrella sampling
  • Machine learning and generative AI (e.g., DDPMs) for property prediction and molecular design
  • Cryo-EM data analysis
  • Structure-based and computer-aided drug design
